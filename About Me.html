About Me...


My Objective

I am an accomplished professional of 18+ years with great interpersonal and communication skills, developed early on in my previous experiences in customer service and perfected in my time as an educator. I revel in finding the root to ongoing challenges while making standard practices and processes more efficient. I aim to bring value to a team of developers as we enhance user experiences, complete software testing, and diagnose development issues and their causes to create viable solutions. I have 10+ years of collaborating with peers, as well as guiding and training inexperienced team members. If anything is constant in the Education field, it is CHANGE, I have been successful learning new technologies by being receptive to change and being hands on, a transferable skill that will bring me success in frontend development.

How did I get here?

Lifelong learner, realist, and lover of creating, researching, and investigating. For most of my life, teaching was the end game. I'm great at it, I love the maths, and I enjoy working with and shaping young adults. Unfortunately, I outgrew the classroom and realized I had potential for more.

In my existential search for a new career path, I stumbled upon a new found interest in coding while building a portfolio website for my (now debunked) plan of transitioning into Instructional Design Learning and Development. I wanted room to grow and moving up into Adult education seemed like an obvious career path for a teacher with tech skills and a general curiosity for technology. I started by learning about Agile, Adult Learning principles, and how to use Articulate StoryLine (which is just fancier Powerpoint). I was really enjoying learning and creating new things, so then I decided that I should have a portfolio to house my Storyline creations.

I researched how to create my own portfolio website, found an HTML5 template, researched how to convert Storyline presentations so I can host them or embed them on my portfolio website, and discovered that I much rather be doing that. While working on my portfolio, I learned a lot of HTML, some CSS, I learned about GitHub and creating repos, and all that jazz. I have always been intrigued by the idea of software development but I never thought that I could venture into that world. So, I readjusted my plan and decided that I should learn how to code. I figured out that Frontend development is where I want to be and started learning from every where and anywhere.

I am looking forward to continuing my journey into the tech industry through continuous learning and improvement. Acquiring knowledge as I go.
